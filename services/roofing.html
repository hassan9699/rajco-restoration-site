Why flat roofs fail in NYC

A flat or low-slope roof only works if water actually leaves it — so drainage is the whole game. A single clogged drain or scupper is enough to let water pool for days, and standing water finds any weak point in the membrane far faster than rain running off a pitched roof ever would. NYC's wide seasonal swings compound the problem: membrane material expands and contracts with every hot summer day and cold winter night, and seams — the joints between membrane sheets — are exactly where that repeated movement eventually opens a gap.

Roof leaks are also notoriously hard to trace from the inside: water entering at one point on the roof can travel along a joist or a layer of insulation before it finally shows up as a stain on a ceiling that's several feet, or even a room, away from the actual failure. That's why a roof repair starts on the roof, not by patching the ceiling underneath it.
