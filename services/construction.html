Why one contractor for the whole exterior

Exterior systems interact more than owners expect — a fence post set too close to a foundation wall complicates future waterproofing access, new siding installed over a gutter problem just traps the water instead of shedding it, and a patio poured against the house without the right slope sends water toward the foundation instead of away from it. Coordinating siding, fencing, patios, retaining walls, and gutters under one contractor means someone is actually thinking about how those pieces interact, instead of five separate trades each solving their own piece in isolation.
