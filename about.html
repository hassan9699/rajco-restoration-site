About Rajco

The repair and the paperwork, handled together

Rajco Restoration Corp does construction and restoration work across New York City, with a focus on the kind of violation repair that most owners get stuck on: not the physical work itself, but the paperwork required to actually clear it.

Why owners get stuck

Getting cited by DOT, DOB, ECB, or HPD is the easy part to understand — a fine shows up, and the meter keeps running until it's resolved. What's less obvious is that fixing the underlying problem isn't enough on its own. Clearing a violation usually means filing something specific with the agency that issued it:

  • Re-inspection filings — for DOT sidewalk violations, the repair has to be re-inspected and confirmed before the violation is dismissed.
  • Certificates of Correction — for HPD and ECB/OATH violations, a formal certification has to be submitted and accepted before the record shows as closed.
  • QEWI sign-off — for Local Law 11 / FISP facade filings, a Qualified Exterior Wall Inspector has to close out the inspection cycle.

Skip that step, and a violation can sit open — accruing fines — even after the repair is finished. We handle the repair and the specific paperwork each agency requires, so nothing falls through that gap.

What we do

Repair work for DOT sidewalk, Local Law 11 / FISP facade, DOB ECB/OATH, and HPD housing violations — plus general restoration and construction work: waterproofing, brickwork, painting, concrete, and interior/exterior construction, whether or not it's tied to an open violation.
