Why owners get stuck on violation jobs

Getting cited by DOT, DOB, ECB, or HPD is the easy part to understand — a fine shows up, and the meter keeps running until it's resolved. What's less obvious is that fixing the underlying problem isn't enough on its own. Clearing a violation usually means filing something specific with the agency that issued it:

  • Re-inspection filings — for DOT sidewalk violations, the repair has to be re-inspected and confirmed before the violation is dismissed.
  • Certificates of Correction — for HPD and ECB/OATH violations, a formal certification has to be submitted and accepted before the record shows as closed.
  • QEWI sign-off — for Local Law 11 / FISP facade filings, a Qualified Exterior Wall Inspector has to close out the inspection cycle.

Skip that step, and a violation can sit open — accruing fines — even after the repair is finished. For violation-driven jobs, we handle the repair and the specific paperwork each agency requires, so nothing falls through that gap.
